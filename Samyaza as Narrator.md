### Narrative Timing Options

#### Modern Day Recollection

- Watches humanity's developed psychological defenses (othering, categorization)
- Sees [[Awan]]'s time as lost paradise of genuine connection
- Elegiac tone, mourning what was lost
- Perspective on how humanity learned to protect through emotional distance

#### Contemporary Narration (His Own Time)

- Witnessing original human consciousness in real-time
- Recognizing something divine in humans that [[God]]'s commandments corrupt
- Explains why Watchers fell - trying to help humans against divine damage

#### Post-Fall/Imprisonment

- Bitter irony - punished for trying to preserve natural human goodness
- Watches humanity struggle with moral confusion from both divine and angelic intervention
- Tells [[Awan]]'s story as testament to what humanity was meant to be

### His Relationship with [[Awan]]

- Possibly her childhood protector/teacher before his fall
- Sees in her the last of original human consciousness
- His care for her makes later corruption of humanity more tragic
- Bridge between divine and human understanding
