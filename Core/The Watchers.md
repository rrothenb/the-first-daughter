## Overview

**The Watchers** (Hebrew: עירין *iyrin*, Aramaic: עִיר *'iyr*, Greek: ἐγρήγοροι *egrḗgoroi* - meaning "wakeful" or "those who are awake") are a group of angels described extensively in 1 Enoch chapters 6-16, also mentioned in Daniel, 2 Enoch, Jubilees, and various rabbinic sources.

**Primary Sources:**
- 1 Enoch (Book of the Watchers, chapters 1-36, especially 6-16)
- Book of Jubilees
- Book of Giants (Dead Sea Scrolls fragments)
- Genesis 6:1-4 (implied connection to "sons of God")
- Daniel 4:13, 17, 23
- 2 Enoch (Slavonic Enoch)

---

## The Descent and Rebellion

### The Conspiracy
According to 1 Enoch 6, **200 Watchers** descended to earth, led by **Samyaza** (also called Shemihazah, Semjaza). They gathered on **Mount Hermon** (called Ardis in some texts) and swore a mutual oath not to abandon their plan.

**1 Enoch 6:3-8 paraphrased:**
- Samyaza feared he alone would bear the guilt of their plan
- All 200 Watchers swore binding oaths together
- They descended to Mount Hermon
- They took human women as wives
- They taught forbidden knowledge

### Their Original Role
Before the fall, the Watchers were:
- Holy angels assigned to watch over humanity
- Meant to guide and protect humans
- Part of the heavenly hierarchy (possibly rank of Seraphim according to some traditions)
- Teachers and intermediaries between heaven and earth

## Named Leaders of the Watchers

### PRIMARY LEADERS

**1. SAMYAZA / Shemihazah (שׁמיחזה)**
- **Name meaning:** "My name has seen" or "He sees the name" or "Infamous rebellion"
- **Role:** Chief leader of the 200 Watchers
- **Status:** Before the fall, possibly of Seraphim rank; some traditions say he was Uzza, tutelary angel of Egypt
- **Actions:** 
  - Organized the descent and oath on Mount Hermon
  - Led the taking of human wives
  - Taught exorcisms and root-cutting (herbalism/pharmacology)
- **Offspring:** In Zohar tradition, fathered Hiwa and Hiya with a daughter of Eve
- **Punishment:** Bound for 70 generations in the valleys of the earth, then cast into the abyss of fire
- **Significance:** Some traditions confuse him with Satan, but scholarly consensus treats them as separate beings
- **Source:** 1 Enoch 6-10, Zohar

**2. AZAZEL / Azael / Asael (עזאזל)**
- **Name meaning:** "Strength of God" or variations
- **Role:** Chief teacher of forbidden arts, sometimes portrayed as co-leader with Samyaza
- **Status:** Emerges as prominent figure, possibly ringleader of corruption
- **Teachings:** 
  - Metallurgy and weaponry (swords, knives, shields, breastplates, coats of mail)
  - Mining and metalwork
  - Cosmetics and beautification (antimony, eye makeup, rouge)
  - Jewelry-making with precious stones
  - All arts of ornamentation
- **Connection:** Name appears in Leviticus 16 as the wilderness demon to whom the scapegoat is sent on Day of Atonement
- **Punishment:** Bound hand and foot, cast into desert pit of Dudael, covered with jagged rocks and darkness, awaits judgment day when cast into fire. All sins of the world written over him.
- **Source:** 1 Enoch 8:1, 10:4-8; Leviticus 16:8-10

### OTHER NAMED WATCHERS (from 1 Enoch 6:7)

**3. ARAQIEL / Arakiel (ארקיאל)**
- **Name meaning:** "Earth of God"
- **Teaching:** Signs of the earth
- **Source:** 1 Enoch 6:7, 8:3

**4. ARMAROS / Amaros**
- **Teaching:** Resolving of enchantments, raising spells
- **Source:** 1 Enoch 6:7, 8:3

**5. BARAQIEL / Baraqijal**
- **Teaching:** Astrology, divination from the stars
- **Source:** 1 Enoch 6:7, 8:3

**6. KOKABIEL / Kawkabel (כוכביאל)**
- **Name meaning:** "Star of God"
- **Teaching:** Astrology, constellations
- **Source:** 1 Enoch 6:7, 8:3

**7. EZEKIEL / Ezeqeel**
- **Teaching:** Augury from the clouds, knowledge of clouds
- **Source:** 1 Enoch 6:7, 8:3

**8. GADREEL**
- **Name meaning:** "Wall of God" (גדר האל)
- **Teaching:** Art of cosmetics, use of weapons, killing blows
- **Significance:** Some traditions say he was the serpent who tempted Eve
- **Source:** 1 Enoch 6:7

**9. PENEMUE**
- **Teaching:** Writing with ink and paper, the "bitter and sweet," secrets of wisdom
- **Significance:** Introduced literacy to humanity
- **Source:** 1 Enoch 69:8

**10. SARIEL / Suriel / Sariel**
- **Teaching:** Courses of the moon (forbidden knowledge)
- **Source:** 1 Enoch 6:7

**11. SHAMSIEL**
- **Teaching:** Signs of the sun
- **Background:** Once a guardian of Eden (Zohar), aide to Uriel
- **Source:** Jubilees, Zohar

**12. YEQON / Jeqon (יָקוּם)**
- Listed among the chiefs
- **Source:** 1 Enoch 6:7

## The Forbidden Teachings

### Categories of Knowledge Transmitted

**1. WARFARE & VIOLENCE**
- Weaponry: swords, knives, shields, armor
- Battle tactics and military strategy
- Killing techniques

**2. METALLURGY & CRAFTS**
- Mining and extraction of metals
- Metalworking and smithing
- Jewelry-making

**3. COSMETICS & BEAUTIFICATION**
- Eye makeup and cosmetics
- Beautification of eyelids
- Use of antimony and rouge
- Hair and body ornamentation
- Precious stones and ornaments

**4. OCCULT KNOWLEDGE**
- Magical medicines
- Incantations and enchantments
- Exorcisms
- Root-cutting (herbalism for magical purposes)
- Sorcery and spells
- Charms

**5. DIVINATION & ASTROLOGY**
- Reading the stars
- Courses of the moon and sun
- Signs of the earth
- Augury from clouds
- Weather prediction
- Celestial observation

**6. WRITING & LITERACY**
- Writing with ink and paper
- Record-keeping
- "Bitter and sweet" knowledge
- Secrets of wisdom

## The Nephilim: Offspring of the Watchers

### Description
**Hebrew:** נְפִילִים (*Nephilim*) - "fallen ones" or "giants"

**Characteristics:**
- Born from unions between Watchers and human women
- Described as "great giants"
- Height: 3,000 ells (approximately 450 feet) according to 1 Enoch 7:2
- Consumed enormous resources: 1,000 camels, horses, and oxen daily per some traditions
- Became violent and oppressive
- Eventually turned to cannibalism

**Actions:**
- Consumed all human resources and acquisitions
- When resources ran out, turned against humanity
- Devoured humans, birds, beasts, reptiles, and fish
- Drank blood (violation of later Noahic covenant)
- Fought civil wars among themselves
- Enslaved humanity

**Notable Named Nephilim:**
- **Hiwa and Hiya**: Sons of Samyaza according to Zohar
- **Og**: King of Bashan, last of the Rephaim, grandson of Samyaza according to some traditions

## Divine Response and Punishment

### The Four Archangels' Intervention

**1. URIEL**
- Mission: Warn Noah of coming flood
- Ensure Noah and his family survive

**2. RAPHAEL**
- Mission: Deal with Azazel
- Actions:
  - Bind Azazel hand and foot
  - Cast him into pit of Dudael in the desert
  - Cover him with jagged rocks and darkness
  - Write all the sins of the world over him (scapegoat motif)
  - Heal the earth from angelic corruption
  - Proclaim healing to restore creation

**3. GABRIEL**
- Mission: Destroy the Nephilim
- Actions:
  - Incite civil war among the giants
  - Cause them to destroy each other
  - Ensure they live short lives
  - Separate them from humanity

**4. MICHAEL**
- Mission: Judge and bind the Watchers
- Actions:
  - Bind Samyaza and his associates
  - Imprison them in valleys of the earth for 70 generations
  - After witnessing destruction of their children, cast them into abyss of fire
  - Execute final judgment

### The Great Flood
- God's ultimate response to cleanse the earth
- Destroys remaining corruption from Watchers' influence
- Preserves only Noah's family
- Marks transition from antediluvian to post-flood world

## Theological Significance

### In 1 Enoch Tradition
- **Cause of the Flood:** Not primarily human sin, but angelic rebellion
- **Origin of Evil:** Explains presence of evil, violence, and forbidden knowledge
- **Eschatology:** Watchers bound until final judgment, then eternally punished
- **Knowledge Ethics:** Distinguishes between appropriate and forbidden knowledge

### In Jewish Tradition
- **Debate over Genesis 6:1-4:** Are "sons of God" the Watchers or righteous line of Seth?
- **Angelic Free Will:** Can angels rebel? Do they have moral agency?
- **Intercessory Role:** Enoch's petition on behalf of Watchers (rejected)
- **Day of Atonement:** Azazel connection to scapegoat ritual

### Variations Across Texts
- **1 Enoch:** Detailed narrative, 200 Watchers, focus on Samyaza and Azazel
- **Jubilees:** Watchers described, but less detail on individual names
- **Book of Giants:** Focuses on offspring and their dreams of destruction
- **2 Enoch:** Places Watchers in fifth heaven, mentions 2 million total
- **Rabbinic sources:** Often spiritualize or reinterpret the story
