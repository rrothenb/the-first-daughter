## Explicit Rules During Awan's Lifetime

   - THE foundational commandment

   - Given to Adam before Eve was created
   - First stated rule, first violated rule

2. **"Be fruitful and multiply"** (Genesis 1:28)
   - Debatable whether it's command or blessing
   - Uses imperative verb forms in Hebrew

   - About reproduction, not moral behavior

3. **"Till the garden and guard it"** (Genesis 2:15)
   - Work mandate
   - Given to Adam in Eden
   - Not clearly a moral command, more job description

### What's Missing

**No Explicit Commands About:**

- Theft
- Lying
- Violence
- Sexual ethics
- Treatment of others
- Justice or fairness
- Respect for life
- Property rights

**The Shocking Reality:**
During Awan's entire lifetime (roughly 75 biblical years / 6-7 actual years), there were **at most 2-3 explicit divine rules** depending on how you count them. Everything else was either:
- Learned through consequences

## The 613 Mitzvot: Origins and Timeline

### When Were They Given?

**The Timeline Gap:**
- **Awan's era:** Years 0-75 (normalized: 0-6.25 years)
- **Exodus from Egypt:** Approximately 400 years after Abraham
- **Mount Sinai:** Shortly after the Exodus
- **Time gap:** Roughly **1,000+ years** after Awan's death

According to Jewish tradition, God gave ALL 613 commandments to Moses at Mount Sinai - more than a millennium after the antediluvian period.

### Which Texts Contain Them?

The 613 mitzvot are scattered throughout the **Five Books of Moses** (Torah/Pentateuch):
1. **Genesis** (Bereishit) - Creation through Joseph
2. **Exodus** (Shemot) - Egypt, Exodus, Sinai revelation, Ten Commandments
3. **Leviticus** (Vayikra) - Priestly laws, sacrifices, purity
4. **Numbers** (Bamidbar) - Wilderness wanderings, more laws
5. **Deuteronomy** (Devarim) - Moses' final speeches, law repetition

They're NOT presented as a single list in the Torah itself.

### The Number 613: A Rabbinic Construction

**When the Number First Appears:**
- **Rabbi Simlai** in a **3rd century CE sermon** (Babylonian Talmud, Makkot 23b)
- That's about **1,500 years** after Moses supposedly received them
- The number is **NOT explicit in the Torah itself**

**His Reasoning:**
- 365 negative commandments (days of solar year)
- 248 positive commandments (bones/limbs in human body)
- Total: 613
- This was **sermonic/homiletic**, not a precise count

**Other Derivations:**
- **Gematria:** Hebrew word for 613 is "taryag" (ת-ר-י-ג)
- Found in Genesis 32:5: Jacob says "I have dwelled (garti)" with Laban
- Same letters, suggesting Jacob kept all 613 commandments even before Sinai
- **Deuteronomy 33:4:** "Moses commanded us the Torah"
- Numerical value (gematria) of "Torah" = 611
- Plus 2 commandments heard directly from God = 613

### The Shocking Truth: No Consensus

**Different Medieval Counts:**
- **Maimonides (Rambam)** - 12th century - most famous enumeration
- **Nachmanides (Ramban)** - disagreed, said it was "rabbinic controversy"
- **Sefer ha-Chinuch** - anonymous, follows Maimonides mostly
- **Ben Azzai** - counted **300** positive mitzvot
- **Menahem Recanati** - counted **611** total
- **Gersonides** - counted **513** total
- **Ibn Ezra** - denied it was authentic tradition, said "there is no end to the number"

**Modern Scholarly Assessment:**
> "Yet, taken as a whole, rabbinic literature hardly supports the claim that the Torah contains 613 commandments and the Babylonian Talmud never takes up the question of what constitutes a commandment for the purposes of this enumeration...there is perhaps no idea that is simultaneously as widely accepted, yet with so little basis in rabbinic literature."

**What's Actually Observ able Today:**
Many commandments only apply to:
- Temple service (which no longer exists)
- Priests (kohanim) specifically
- Men only
- Women only
- People living in Israel
- Specific situations (like Nazirite vows)

According to one count, only **77 positive** and **194 negative** commandments can even be observed today - about **271 total**, not 613.

### Implications for Your Novel

**For Awan's World:**
1. The 613 mitzvot **didn't exist yet** - they came 1,000+ years later
2. Even the **10 Commandments didn't exist** - also given at Sinai
3. The "rules" in her era were **minimal** - just what we've identified
4. The number 613 itself is a **later rabbinic construct** - invented in a sermon 1,500+ years after the commandments were supposedly given
5. There's **no scholarly consensus** on what the 613 even are

**The Reality:**
During Awan's lifetime, there was an **extreme paucity of explicit divine rules**. The massive legal code came much, much later - and even then, scholars can't agree on exactly what it contains.

This reinforces your portrayal of people trying to figure out morality with minimal divine guidance, under an unpredictable authority figure who reveals rules primarily through punishment rather than clear instruction.

## God's After-the-Fact Preferences and Hidden Rules

### Rules Revealed Only Through Consequences

**1. Abel's Offering Preferred Over Cain's** (Genesis 4:4-5)

**What the Text Says:**
- Abel brought "the choicest of the firstlings of his flock" (quality noted)
- Cain brought "fruit of the ground" (no quality descriptor)
- God accepted Abel's, rejected Cain's
- **NO REASON GIVEN**

**What's NOT in the Text:**
- No prior instruction to make offerings
- No specification of what kind of offering
- No quality standards established
- No explanation for the preference

**Later Speculations:**
- Blood vs. no blood?
- Firstfruits vs. leftovers?
- Heart attitude?
- Faith? (per Hebrews 11:4, written much later)

**The Problem:**
God's preference is revealed only by reaction. No rule had been stated. Many assume God must have told them to sacrifice, but **the text never says that**.

**2. Murder is Wrong** (Genesis 4:8-16)

**No Prior Prohibition:**
- No "thou shalt not kill" before Abel's death
- God's condemnation comes AFTER the act
- He curses Cain, marks him, makes him a wanderer
- The wrongness of murder is demonstrated by punishment, not prior prohibition

**3. Violence and "Corruption" Are Displeasing** (Genesis 6:5-13)

**Before the Flood, God Sees:**
- "The wickedness of man was great"
- "Every intent of the thoughts of his heart was only evil continually"
- "The earth was corrupt"
- "The earth was filled with violence"

**The Unstated Standards:**
- What constituted this "corruption"?
- What specific acts counted as "violence"?
- Were these violations of stated rules or just divine displeasure?
- The text never specifies what they were doing wrong

**4. Garments of Skin** (Genesis 3:21)

**God Makes Clothing:**
- After the Fall, God makes "garments of skins" for Adam and Eve
- This implies an animal was killed
- Some interpret this as God's first sacrifice/atonement
- But no rule about clothing had been stated
- No command to cover themselves

**5. Enoch "Walked with God"** (Genesis 5:22-24)

**Mysterious Approval:**
- Enoch "walked with God" and was taken
- This implies he pleased God somehow
- But what did "walking with God" mean?
- What did he do that was right?
- No specifics given

**6. Noah "Found Favor"** (Genesis 6:8)

**Selective Survival:**
- Noah "found favor in the eyes of the Lord"
- He's called "righteous" and "blameless"
- But by what standard?
- What did he do differently from everyone else?
- The text doesn't specify

### The Pattern

**What This Reveals:**
God operates on unstated preferences and retroactive revelation:
- Standards exist but aren't communicated
- People discover rules by violating them
- Punishment teaches what prohibition should have
- Some people randomly find favor, others don't
- No clear path to divine approval

## The Noahide Laws: Questionable Documentation

### The Traditional Claim

**Seven Laws Given to Adam/Noah:**
1. Establish courts of justice
2. No blasphemy
3. No idolatry
4. No sexual immorality
5. No murder
6. No theft
7. No eating flesh from a living animal (added after Noah)

**The Claim:**
The first six were given to Adam, all seven to Noah.

### Where's the Documentation?

**Primary Source:**
**Babylonian Talmud, Sanhedrin 56a-b**
- States: "According to the Talmud, the seven laws were given first to Adam and subsequently to Noah"
- Derives these from Genesis 2:16 where God commands Adam

**The Problems:**

1. **Not in Genesis Text:**
   - Genesis 2:16 says: "And the Lord God commanded the man, saying, 'Of every tree of the garden you may freely eat'"
   - This is about the tree, not about seven laws
   - The Talmudic rabbis were **interpreting/deriving**, not reading explicitly

2. **Post-Facto Justification:**
   - "The generality of the verse and rival opinions citing other texts indicate that this text is probably only post-facto support for the concept"
   - Rabbinic interpretation, not biblical text

3. **No Universal Revelation:**
   - "No explicit universal revelation of these commandments occurs in the Bible"
   - They're **derived from reason or natural law** according to some Jewish thinkers

4. **Talmudic, Not Biblical:**
   - The documentation exists **in the Talmud** (Sanhedrin 56a-b)
   - Written centuries after the biblical period
   - Rabbinic interpretation of what God *must have* said
   - Not what the text actually says

### What This Means for Your Novel

**The Reality:**
The Noahide laws are a **rabbinical construction** attempting to fill the uncomfortable gap of "what rules existed before Sinai?" They're reading back into Genesis what they think should have been there.

**For Awan:**
She would NOT have had access to these neat seven principles. At most, she had:
- Don't eat from the tree (stated)
- Be fruitful and multiply (stated, sort of)
- Rules learned through consequences (murder, corruption, violence - all defined after the fact)

The idea that there was a clear seven-law framework is later theological retrofit, not textual reality.

## Learned Helplessness Framework

### Psychological Research Application

**Learned Helplessness Defined:**
When organisms (including humans) repeatedly experience that **outcomes are independent of their responses** - that nothing they do changes what happens - they develop:
- Expectation of uncontrollability in new situations
- Cessation of trying
- Stress, depression, PTSD
- Physical health problems
- Difficulty in relationships throughout life

**The Classic Experiments:**
Research by Martin Seligman showed that when:
- Punishment is unpredictable
- Responses don't reliably affect outcomes
- No clear connection between behavior and consequences
- The subject develops helplessness, anxiety, depression

**The "Nothing I Do Matters" Effect:**
The most devastating aspect: repeated experience that outcomes are independent of responses creates:
- Expectation of uncontrollability
- Cessation of trying
- Chronic stress and depression
- Physical health deterioration
- Relationship difficulties throughout life

### Application to Genesis

**What God Does:**
- Accepts Abel's offering, rejects Cain's with no clear reason stated beforehand
- Punishes eating from the tree (consequences come later)
- Doesn't state murder is wrong before Abel's death
- Sends the flood for "corruption" and "violence" without specific rules violated
- Takes Enoch without explanation
- Creates environment where people can't predict what pleases or angers God

**Psychological Effects on Awan's Generation:**
- **Learned helplessness:** "Nothing we do makes God happy/safe"
- **Hypervigilance:** Constantly watching for divine disapproval
- **Anxiety:** Never knowing what might trigger punishment
- **Insecurity:** Unable to develop stable sense of self
- **Difficulty with relationships:** If you can't trust the primary authority figure, who can you trust?
- **Complex trauma:** Living under unpredictable, all-powerful authority who might destroy you at any moment
- **Confusion about morality:** No clear guidelines to develop ethical sense

**This Isn't Speculation:**
These are well-documented psychological effects of growing up under inconsistent, unpredictable, arbitrary authority. Decades of research on what happens to children raised in exactly these conditions.

**The Implications:**
Your framing of God as narcissistic/abusive is supported by psychological research on authority figures who:
